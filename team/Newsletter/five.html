BUSINESS & TECH
If you’re looking for a Halloween scare, look no further than this week's Q3 earnings. As requested on our nifty feedback form, readers are looking to learn more about IPOs, startups, stocks, and the business side of tech. So here we are, with an all-new BUSINESS & TECH section.

Q3 reports: October wraps with small U.S. stock jumps


It has been a notoriously bad month for investors around the world. Stocks have lost > $5 trillion. Additionally, a drop in bond prices pushed yields higher than ever, leaving investors no safety net. Tech was one of the most sold sectors in October. Although NASDAQ (a tech heavy stock exchange) increased 2.1%, it is still on track for its worst month since the 2008 Recession.
  • Facebook's earnings were announced Tuesday. Facebook's daily users stayed at around 185 million and the sales were weaker than expected - but the lack of growth was not a huge concern due to low expectations. The Q3 earnings report instead resulted in a stock climb of around 4%.
  • Alphabet, Google's parent company, reported Q3 earnings last Thursday. It beat earnings estimates but failed to meet on revenue expectations. As a result, the stock sunk around 5%. Google's cloud business represents its slowest pace of growth since 2016. Google's market share is far behind Amazon and Microsoft.
  • Amazon's third quarter earnings beat most estimates, but the revenue failed to meet expectations. As a result, the stock plunged 10%, which is the company's largest decline since early 2014. Overall, Amazon has seen slowing growth. The fourth quarter is not looking optimistic; the results show the marginal cloud and advertising businesses are growing faster than the core retail business.
  • Apple will report their Q3 earnings this Thursday. It is expected to post revenue of around $61.34 billion, which is an earnings per share of $2.78. After seven quarters of accelerated growth rates, Apple is under a lot of pressure. Expectations hint at a double digit growth rate.
-- Veena Ramesh
