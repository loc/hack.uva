Take-aways from last week's events

Pariveda held an info session about technological consulting - they went into detail about the industry in general, jobs specifics and opportunities for students at UVA, followed by a Q+A. We asked 4th-year CS major David Zhao what his 3 takeaways from the session were:

1) As a tech consultant, you'll be able to learn and use the newest and coolest technologies before they're publicly available.

2) It seems like a great place to work. Specifically, the Pariveda employees mentioned how much the company cares about you as a person. For example, if you don't like your travel schedule, you can voice that and things will change.

3) Ben & Jerry’s tastes better when its free.




Venture for America’s How to Identify a Startup Idea went swimmingly; here's what one of the organisers had to say about it:

The VFA workshop we hosted on Thursday was designed to encourage a small group of students to engage in creative, hands-on problem solving by introducing them to a startup mindset. The workshop host, VFA fellow Winson Law, started by posing a problem to solve -- dining on campus. We carefully worked through strategies and post-it brainstorming in teams to address the problem, step by step - from identifying specific problem-areas, all the way to designing business models for a startup around selected solutions. All in all, it was a fantastic event that really exposed students to thinking like an entrepreneur!
